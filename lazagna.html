"Meat" Sause

Essential Ingredients

It is a good time to be a vegan becasue... plant-based meat alternatives are a thing. There are a bunch of different choices, and the best one(s) for you really depend on your personal taste. If you want to know more about plant-based burgers and the different brands out there that you should try, check out this article by the NY Times for some reviews to help you get started.

We chose to use the Impossible Burger for this recipe becasue 1) its one of the best plant-based ground-beef substitues out there and 2) it doesn't have that strong taste profile that some of the other brands, like the Beyond Burger, have. Beyond is my favorite brand for hamburgers precisely becasue of this reason - you don't have to add any fancy ingredients to make it taste good - it comes that way already! But for our purposes, a more subtle flavor is preferable.

The sausage is not a neccessary ingredient - you can forgo it and add more ground-beef or a chunkier tomato sause later on. But, if you like a little extra heat and/or you want a little more flavor in the sause, we recomend it!The Beyond Meat Hot Sausage is our favorite.

Mix it Up

Place the plant-based meat into the pot, separate it using a spoon or spatula, and let cook, uncovered, for about 7 minutes or until brown, stirring occasionally.

Once the plant-based "meat" has browned, add the garlic and onions to and continue to mix together. Lower the heat on the pot to a simmer so that nothing sticks to the bottom, and make sure to keep sitring! Yes, it gets a little annoying... push through it.


The last step of this is to add the tomato sause and some seasoning. We also added some cherry tomatos becasue they are delicious and we had some extra cherry tomatos in our fridge. Turn off the burner, add the seasoning, and stir together.

The resulting sause should be the consistency of a thick soup, but not too watery. If the consistency is not right, add some additional ingredients to fix it. If it is too watery, keep the pot on a low simmer and stir until reaching the right consistency. If it is too thick, just add some additional sause (if you have any) or water if not.

Putting it All Together

Almost done! The harest part is behind you now - no more stirring required.


Layering

Time to put the cheese, meat sause and pasta together.

This is when you need to get a little creative. I would recommend adding at least one kind (if I had to only choose one: Miyokos Creamery Organic Vegan Mozzarella Nut Cheese). However, we also listed a few extra cheeses becasue we had some in our fridge.

Cook the pasta. You want it to be al dente, so follow the instructions on the box for the correct cooking time. Drain the pasta and pull out the baking pan.

Layer the Lasagne, swaping between meat sause, cashew cheese sause, assorted cheeses, and pasta, until the pan is about a centimeter from the top. Do this for both baking pans.

Top it off with a tick layer of cheese and sause - be generous, you can't have too much cheese!


Bake

Cover the pans with aluminum foil and fold-down on all sides. Place the pans in the over at 350 for 30 min to cook the inside and heat the cheese. Take the pans out, uncover, and place back in the oven to cook for an additional 10 min to crisp the top.

The Final Product

Cut the Lasagne (the size is up to you), plate it, eat and enjoy! Add fresh parsley or basil if you feel like adding a little extra flourish.

Traditional Lasagne - Vegan Style

31

A savory meat and cheese Lasagne that tastes like the real thing!

Author: Nate Nuval
Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 1 h 10 min
Yield: Serves 12-16

INGREDIENTS

    Cashews Cheese Sause

  • 1 ½ Cup Cashews
  • 1 Cup Water
  • 1 tbsp Apple Cider Vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Nutritional Yeast
  • 1 tbsp Garlic Powder
  • 1 tbsp Onion Powder

    • "Meat" Sause

  • 1 White Onion
  • 2 Cloves Garlic
  • 1-2 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 12oz Plant-Based Ground Beef
  • 4 3.5oz Plant-Based Sausage
  • ½ tsp Basil
  • ½ tsp Italian Seasoning
  • ½ tsp Thyme
  • 1-2 tsp Salt
  • 1-2 tsp Ground Pepper
  • 24oz (3 Cups) Tomato Sause
  • 1 ½ - 2 cups Sliced Cherry Tomatos

    • Pasta & Cheese

  • 16oz Lasagne Pasta
  • ⅓ Cup Follow Your Heart Parmesan Style Grated Cheese Alternative
  • ½ Cup Daiya Dairy-Free Shredded Cheddar Cheese
  • 4oz Miyokos Creamery Organic Vegan Mozzarella Nut Cheese

INSTRUCTIONS

Soak cashews for 3-5 hours in water. If you are limited on time, bring a smaller pot of water to boil, pour over cashews, and let sit for 30 minutes. Do not drain water.

Blend together the cashews, (cashew) water, apple cider vinegar, nutritional yeast garlic powder, and onion powder until smooth. Put aside.

Cut onion into small peices (~1 cm long). Cut garlic finely. Set both aside.

Add oil to pot and turn to medium-low heat. Add package of plant-based ground-beef and plant-based sausage. Use spatula to break apart "meat" into fine peices and cook, stiring continuously, until browned (about 7 minutes).

Turn heat to low. Add onions and garlic basil, Italian seasoning, thyme, salt and ground pepper to miture and stir together well. Add tomato sause and sliced cherry tomatos to mixture and stir together. Let simmer for 20-30 minutes.

Pre-heat over to 350Â° F. In a 3-5 inch tall pan, start layering meat sause, cashew cheese sause, a sprinkling of vegan cheese, and pasta. Stop about 1 cm from the top. Use a second pan if needed.

Cover the pans in aluminum foil. Place in over and cook for 30 minutes. Take pan out, uncover, and place back in the oven to cook for another 10 minutes.

Cut into segements, eat, and enjoy!
